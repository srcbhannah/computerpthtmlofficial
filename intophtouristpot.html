tradition

Pasko, or Christmas in the Philippines, is a deeply cherished tradition celebrated with vibrant festivities, strong faith, and family gatherings. Known for having the longest Christmas season in the world, it begins as early as September with the "ber months." Filipinos decorate homes with colorful parols (star lanterns), sing carols, and attend Simbang Gabi, a series of dawn masses leading up to Christmas Day. The celebration is marked by sharing hearty feasts, exchanging gifts, and spreading joy, reflecting the Filipino spirit of love, unity, and generosity during the holiday season.
